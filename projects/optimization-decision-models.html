Operations Optimization & Decision Models

A six-part operations and supply chain project applying linear programming, queueing theory, mixed-integer models, Monte Carlo simulation, payoff & regret tables, and EMV decision trees to real-world scenarios in retail, legal services, and manufacturing.

Resource Optimization Quantitative Decision-Making

Project Narrative

These six business problems were solved using operations research techniques rooted in optimization, probability, and decision science. Each model targets efficiency, profitability, or risk reduction, demonstrating how structured analytics directly support real-world decisions.

The combined set forms a decision-support toolkit applicable across retail operations, inventory management, legal strategy, labor planning, and manufacturing assignments.

What I Built

  • Queueing model for retail checkout optimization.
  • Linear programming model for copier cost minimization.
  • Binary integer programming for machinist–machine assignment.
  • Monte Carlo simulation for uncertain profitability.
  • Payoff & regret tables for perishable inventory decisions.
  • EMV decision tree for legal strategy optimization.

Optimization Models (Queueing, Linear Programming, Binary Assignment)

Included Models:

  • Queueing Model: Determined cashier staffing using M/M/c queues.
  • LP Copier Model: Compared copier leasing options for minimum daily cost.
  • Binary Assignment: Assigned machinists to machines using a BILP.

Representative Equation (Queueing):

ρ = λ / (cμ)

Select minimum c such that:
P(wait > 5 min) ≤ threshold

Representative Equation (BILP):

Minimize Σᵢ Σⱼ (timeᵢⱼ · xᵢⱼ)

Subject to:
Σⱼ xᵢⱼ = 1
Σᵢ xᵢⱼ = 1
xᵢⱼ ∈ {0,1}

Simulation Models (Monte Carlo Profit Analysis)

A Monte Carlo simulation estimated expected profit and uncertainty for a new product launch. Each trial generated random cost components, producing a probability distribution for per-unit profit.

  • 20 simulation runs with random draws for cost variables.
  • Mean profit per unit: $6.90.
  • Profit range (95% CI): roughly $4.46–$9.34.
profitᵢ = SellingPrice 
           - purchase_costᵢ

Decision Models (Payoff, Regret, EMV & Decision Trees)

These models evaluate decisions under uncertainty using payoff tables, regret metrics, and expected monetary value. Used for inventory stocking and legal strategy.

  • Inventory decision using payoff + regret + sensitivity.
  • Patent lawsuit decision using EMV-based decision tree.
  • Evaluation of optimistic, pessimistic, and expected value criteria.
EMV(option) = Σ [P(outcomeᵢ) · payoffᵢ]

Choose option with highest EMV.
