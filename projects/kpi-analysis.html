Operational Performance: Manufacturing KPI Analysis

Excel • KPI dashboards • Operations analysis

This project focuses on building an end-to-end operational KPI analysis for a lawn equipment manufacturer. The goal was to assess production performance, identify inefficiencies, and provide management with a clear understanding of trends across on-time delivery, transmission unit cost, and employee retention.

Using Excel, I cleaned multiple raw data sources, created relational connections, calculated key metrics, and built an interactive KPI dashboard that highlights underperforming areas and gives leadership insight into both short-term and long-term operational health.

Data & Methodology

  • Raw CSV and Excel files from operational logs and production tables.
  • Standardized inconsistent fields and removed duplicated rows.
  • Created calculated fields for on-time delivery, unit manufacturing cost, and retention rate.
  • Used pivot tables and slicers to enable multi-view analysis.
  • Designed a management-ready dashboard summarizing performance trends.

Key KPIs Calculated

  • On-Time Delivery % – Percentage of customer orders shipped on or before the promised date.
  • Transmission Unit Cost – Total cost per unit, including labor, parts, overhead, and rework.
  • Retention Rate – Month-over-month employee retention within production lines.
  • Defect Rate – Defective units as a percentage of total production.
  • Product Line Contribution – Unit margin by product type to identify profit drivers.

Sample Excel Logic

# On-Time Delivery %
=IF([@[Ship Date]] <= [@[Due Date]], 1, 0)

# Retention Rate
=([@[Employees Start]] - [@[Employees Separated]]) / [@[Employees Start]]

# Transmission Unit Cost
=[@[Direct Labor]] + [@[Parts Cost]] + [@[Overhead]] + [@[Rework Cost]]

Dashboard Insights

  • The on-time delivery trend showcased strong seasonality and highlighted months where bottlenecks slowed fulfillment.
  • Unit cost analysis revealed specific product lines with declining margins due to higher rework costs.
  • Retention metrics showed correlations between turnover and production dips in high-volume months.
  • The dashboard enabled leadership to identify cost outliers and forecast staffing needs.

Business Value Delivered

This analysis strengthened operational decision-making by giving leadership a clear, consolidated view of production performance. Instead of relying on scattered spreadsheets and unclear trends, the KPI dashboard unified the data into one actionable report.

  • Identified underperforming product lines.
  • Improved ability to forecast inventory and staffing.
  • Highlighted operational inefficiencies costing the company money.
  • Provided a measurable view of manufacturing health.
