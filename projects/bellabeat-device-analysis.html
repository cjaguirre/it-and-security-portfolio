BellaBeat Smart Device Usage Analysis

A full behavioral analysis of public Fitbit activity and sleep data to understand how users engage with smart wellness devices. The findings support BellaBeat’s product strategy by highlighting the habits, routines, and lifestyle markers that predict consistent device engagement.

User Segmentation Activity Behavior

Project Narrative

BellaBeat relies heavily on habitual device engagement. This project examines Fitbit data to uncover the activity, sleep, and movement behaviors that differentiate high-engagement users from those who interact with their devices only occasionally.

The analysis identifies the lifestyle signals that correlate with strong engagement and highlights opportunities for BellaBeat to improve retention through smarter notifications and personalized goals.

What I Built

  • Cleaned and combined Fitbit activity and sleep datasets.
  • Segmented users by activity tiers and engagement consistency.
  • Created visuals for steps, activity minutes, sleep behavior, and calorie trends.
  • Defined insights for product design and wellness recommendations.
  • Mapped clear opportunities for engagement growth.

Dataset & Tools

The analysis used Fitbit activity, sleep, and daily movement data across 12,000+ records. Metrics captured included intensity minutes, total steps, calories, time asleep, and sleep efficiency. Python managed the cleaning, transformation, and summaries.

  • 12k+ rows of Fitbit device usage data
  • Activity intensity and energy expenditure
  • Sleep logs and sleep efficiency
  • Daily steps and calorie totals
  • Python, pandas, seaborn, Matplotlib

Key Findings

  • High-engagement users consistently exceed 10,000 steps per day.
  • Sleep duration of ~6–7 hours correlated with stronger device-usage patterns.
  • Sedentary minutes served as the strongest negative predictor of engagement.
  • Calories and active minutes rose proportionally with step tiers.

Sample Python Code

import pandas as pd

df = pd.read_csv("dailyActivity.csv")

# Summaries
df_summary = df.groupby("Id")[["TotalSteps", "SedentaryMinutes", "Calories"]].mean()

# Categorize users by activity
df['activity_level'] = pd.cut(
    df['TotalSteps'],
    bins=[0, 5000, 10000, 20000],
    labels=['Low activity', 'Moderate activity', 'High activity']
